Fochville residents accuse municipality of toying with their emotions over 'disruptive' water supply crisis

  • Residents of Kokosi Township in Fochville, West Rand, are experiencing a month-long water supply crisis.
  • The community protested on September 23 by marching to the N12 highway, demanding restoration of water supply or the resignation of Merafong Local Municipality mayor Nozuko Best.
  • Prior to the protest, the municipality partially restored water supply but then cut it off again on September 25, causing frustration among residents.
  • Buti Elliot Mtembu, speaking for residents, accused Merafong Local Municipality and Rand Water of playing political games with the community's water supply.
  • Residents have received no clear explanation for the inconsistent water supply from the municipality or Rand Water.
  • Municipality spokesperson Thabo Maloja denied accusations that municipal leaders sabotaged protests or manipulated water supply, emphasizing ongoing dialogue and commitment to resolving water issues.
  • Rand Water did not respond to requests for comment at the time of reporting.
  • The situation matters because access to reliable water is a basic service essential to health and daily living, and the community feels their rights and dignity are being undermined.
